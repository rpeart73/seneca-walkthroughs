Diary Writing Is Not Reflective Learning

A diary records what happened. Reflective learning does more: it moves from the event to what the event shows, and then to a next action. The same writing habit can stay at the level of memory or it can become learning, and the difference is the reflective step. Reflective writing is treated here as a resilience practice, not a private disclosure task.

Insight

A diary stores the event; a reflective journal turns the event into learning by naming what it shows and choosing what to try next. The reflective step is the difference.

Think about it

Think of a recent diary-style note you might write. What would change if you added the question: what does this show, and what will I try next?

Journaling and Critical Thinking

Reflective journaling does more than record learning; it can surface and develop critical thinking dispositions, the habits of mind a learner brings to a problem. Writing reflectively asks a student to question, weigh, and connect, which is why journaling and critical thinking are studied together. The disposition is not a grade or a speed; it is a habit that the journal can make visible.

Insight

A reflective journal can surface and develop critical thinking dispositions, the habits of mind a learner brings to a problem, not just a record of what happened.

Key Terms to Know

Reflection
Reflection is the work that turns experience into learning rather than memory alone. Recording an event keeps it; reflecting on it asks what the event shows and what to do next. This is the central move of the week, given shape by the frame what happened, what it shows, what I will try next.
Reflective journal
A reflective journal is writing that moves from an event to its meaning to a next action, rather than only recording what happened. Treated here as a resilience practice, it can surface and develop critical thinking and connect a personal experience to a course concept without oversharing.
Critical thinking disposition
A critical thinking disposition is a habit of mind a learner brings to a problem, such as questioning, weighing, and connecting. Reflective journaling can surface and develop these dispositions, which is why journaling and critical thinking are studied together. It is a habit, not a grade or a speed.
