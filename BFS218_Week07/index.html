What You Are Learning This Week

Key takeaways from this review
1

Engineered Inequity Amplifies

Engineered inequity is a design that amplifies an existing gap on purpose. The key word is amplify, not create. The harm is active and built into the design.

2

Default Discrimination Rides the Defaults

Default discrimination is harm carried by the defaults: the settings, data, and assumptions that treat one group's world as normal. The key question is whether the glitch is systemic.

3

Coded Exposure Hands Out Visibility Unevenly

Coded exposure is the uneven, designed distribution of visibility. Some people are watched too closely, others are not seen at all. The key question is whether visibility is a trap.

4

Three Dimensions, One System, Real Cases

These are not separate machines. One system can show all three at once, and we found all three in Canada, in Clearview and the RCMP, in Ewert v Canada, and at the digital border.

Key Terms to Know

Tap or click a card (or press Enter) to reveal the definition.
Engineered inequity (dimension one)
Technology that, by its design, amplifies an existing hierarchy of race, class, or gender. The harm is active and built in. The key word is amplify, not create.
Default discrimination (dimension two)
Harm that arrives through the defaults, the settings, data, and assumptions that treat one group's world as normal. The key question is: is the glitch systemic?
Coded exposure (dimension three)
The uneven, designed distribution of visibility: some people watched too closely, others not seen at all. The key question is: is visibility a trap?
